How to Measure for a Shirt

1. Collar Size

Measure Around Your Neck: Wrap a measuring tape around the base of your neck where the collar would sit. Ensure the tape is snug but not tight.

Add Comfort Allowance: Insert one or two fingers between the tape and your neck to add a bit of comfort room.

Record the Measurement: Note down the measurement in inches or centimeters.

2. Chest Size

Measure the Fullest Part of Your Chest: Wrap the measuring tape around the widest part of your chest, typically just under the armpits and across the shoulder blades.

Ensure a Relaxed Fit: Make sure your arms are relaxed at your sides and the tape is parallel to the floor.

Record the Measurement: Note down the measurement in inches or centimeters.

3. Shoulder Size

Identify Shoulder Points: Find the points where your shoulder meets your arm.

Measure Across Your Shoulders: Measure the distance between these two shoulder points, across the back.

Record the Measurement: Note down the measurement in inches or centimeters.

4. Shirt Length

Determine Starting Point: Start from the highest point of your shoulder, close to the base of your neck.

Measure Down the Torso: Measure down to the point where you want the shirt to end, typically around the middle of the hips for a regular fit.

Record the Measurement: Note down the measurement in inches or centimeters.

Tips for Accurate Measurements

Use a Soft Measuring Tape: For flexibility and accuracy, use a soft cloth measuring tape.

Stand Naturally: Stand upright and relaxed while measuring.

Get Assistance: If possible, have someone assist you for more precise measurements.

Double-Check: Take each measurement at least twice to ensure accuracy.
